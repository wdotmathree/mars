Criminal Justice

About the criminal justice system

    The criminal justice system will be focused on getting the right combination of rehabilitation and retribution in order to maximize the pros of both while minimizing the cons. When a person is caught doing a crime they will be sent to court (based on severity and repeat offences). The officer may decide to use extrajudicial measures instead of sending the offender to court for minor and non-repeat offences which decreases load on the court system. When the offender gets to court, they will be judged by an unbiased judge and a jury which will be composed of regular citizens of various backgrounds. Using a (not so) carefully calculated statistic, it can be shown that there will only be about 25 court cases per day across the entire colony. So as long as the systems are designed with that in mind, they will not be overloaded. However, in the case where the court systems cannot be built to handle the load, more resources will be dedicated to rehabilitating youth offenders while the court may have to resort to a more retribution focused approach. This is because rehabilitating youth offenders has been shown to be more effective than adult offenders so the resources are better used there. This should only be used as a last resort however as a combination of rehabilitation and retribution is better than only one or the other in all cases.

Rights and Freedoms

Democratic Rights

   Citizens will have the right to vote for members of the government every 5 years. This allows citizens to take part in decision making of the colony and increases the difficulty of establishing a dictatorship but elections are quite expensive and holding them every 5 years is resource demanding.

Mobility Rights

   Citizens will have the right to move anywhere in the colony but will have to apply to move between planets. This makes it so the citizens will not complain from being restricted to a small region of the colony but will also prevent overbooking and help with pooling people for the interplanatary transportation.

Right to Essential Resources

   Citizens will have the right to essential resources such as food, water, oxygen, etc... They will be freely provided to all citizens to ensure the citizens can survive and work for the colony as if all the citizens become unable to work, the colony will be unable to sustain itself.

Right to Free Healthcare

   Citizens have the right to free healthcare to increase life expectancy and avoid problems such as negative population growth and to keep the citizens healthy and more efficient workers. This would however mean the government has to pay for the healthcare and will thus need to collect more taxes but I believe that this is a worthy trade to make.

Freedom of Expression

   Citizens will be free to speak as they wish, providing they do not violate other laws regarding interpersonal communications. For example, they will still be liable for defamation, spreading disinformation, hate speech, or threats. This also allows the press to freely report on current events and limit the government’s power in possible censorship. This also leads to the citizens being more informed due to the more abundant news sources and encourages the government to fix issues rather than ignoring them.

Equality Rights

   The citizens cannot be discriminated against for no reasons. This aims to decrease the discrimination in the colony and give everyone the same opportunity. However, this is very difficult to enforce and there is a large legal “grey area”. For example, it is reasonable for an employer to hire someone with more specifications over someone with less but the candidate may decide that it is discriminatory and sue the company. On the other hand, the employer may actually be discriminating against the candidates and play it off as choosing someone who “fits better with the company”.
